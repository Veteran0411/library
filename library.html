City central Library:

Establishment of Belagavi City central Library

The Karnataka Government introduced the Public Library Act in 1965 to promote knowledge and interest in books and libraries among citizens. In line with this act, the Belagavi City Library Association was established on 10/7/1970 and a central library was inaugurated on 29/12/1974 on Maruti Street. Later, on 25/9/1989, the library was relocated to its current premises at the District Commissioner's office. Today, the Belagavi Municipal Corporation oversees the operations of the central library, 24 branch libraries, and one mobile library.
Public libraries contain a wealth of knowledge that can benefit individuals of all ages. They provide access to resources like the constitution, which are free for everyone to use, and encourage citizens to take an active role in nation-building. Reading is an integral part of our culture, and it should be incorporated into our daily routines. By reading, individuals can expand their horizons and gain valuable insights into various subjects. Public libraries play a vital role in disseminating knowledge and guiding people from ignorance to enlightenment. They are evolving to become more scientific and sophisticated, and are also contributing positively to society by fostering human connections.
